Wuhan Three-Day Itinerary: Unveiling Beauty, History, and Modernity

This itinerary explores the best of Wuhan, split across the Yangtze River's south and north sides.

Go-To-Tour-Route

Day 1: Unveiling the Beauty and History of the South Bank

  • Morning (3-4 hours): East Lake(东湖) Exploration
    • Begin your day by immersing yourself in the captivating East Lake, Wuhan's crown jewel. Rent a bicycle and meander along the scenic trails bordering the lake, or opt for a peaceful boat ride for a unique perspective.
    • Explore the Wuhan Botanical Garden, a haven for nature enthusiasts, boasting a diverse collection of flora. Pack a picnic lunch to savor amidst the picturesque scenery.
  • Afternoon (2-3 hours): Unveiling Hubei's Rich Past at the Museum(湖北省博物馆)
    • Delve into Hubei's fascinating history at the renowned Hubei Provincial Museum. Explore the vast collection exceeding 200,000 artifacts, spanning from prehistoric times to the present day.
    • Look out for prized exhibits like the Sword of Goujian, an ancient weapon believed to be over 2,400 years old, and the captivating Bianzhong (chime bells). The museum offers English descriptions for many exhibits, enhancing your understanding. Remember to check the museum's opening hours before your visit.
  • Evening: A Touch of Modernity: Chuhehanjie(楚河汉街)
    • Head to Chuhehanjie, a modern marvel on the south bank. This 1.5-kilometer pedestrian street is a shopper's paradise, lined with international brands, local boutiques, and trendy restaurants. Soak in the vibrant atmosphere, browse the shops, or grab a coffee and people-watch.

Day 2: Unveiling More of Wuhan's Charm and History

This itinerary focuses on exploring historical sites and experiencing the Yangtze River while offering a budget-friendly alternative to the Yellow Crane Tower.

  • Morning: Indulge in Local Delights at Liangdao street(粮道街)
    • Start your day with a delicious breakfast at Liangdaojie, a popular street known for its diverse selection of Wuhan breakfast options (guozǎo).
  • Mid-Morning: Explore the Enchanting Tanhualin(昙华林)
    • Tanhualin is known as the first cultural and artistic street in Wuhan. The architectural style of Tanhualin is unique, blending Chinese and Western elements, embodying both the charm of traditional Chinese culture and the characteristics of Western architecture.
  • Afternoon (2-3 hours): Yellow Crane Tower(黄鹤楼) or Memorial of Wuchang Uprising 1911 Revolution(辛亥革命武昌起义纪念馆)
    • Yellow Crane Tower (Optional): If you're willing to splurge, consider visiting the iconic Yellow Crane Tower, a symbol of Wuhan. Climb to the top for panoramic views of the city and the Yangtze River.
    • Memorial of Wuchang Uprising 1911 Revolution (Alternative): For a budget-friendly option, head to the Memorial of Wuchang Uprising 1911 Revolution. This museum commemorates a pivotal event in Chinese history, the Wuchang Uprising, which marked the end of imperial China.
  • Evening: Witness the Majesty of the Yangtze River
    • Take a taxi or ride-sharing service to the Hu Bu Xiang Han Wei Xiao Chi Jie(户部巷) and the Wuhan Ferry Zhonghua Road Marina(中华路码头).
    • Ferry Ride to Wuhanguan from Zhonghua Gate Port: During the journey, witness some of Wuhan's most iconic landmarks, such as the imposing Yellow Crane Tower and the magnificent Wuhan Yangtze River Bridge.
  • Evening (3-4 hours): Wuhan River Beach(江滩公园) and Night Light Show
    • Head to Wuhan Jiangtan Park. Explore the park's gardens and walkways. In the evening, witness the park come alive with a captivating night light show, featuring dazzling illuminations and music.

Day 3: Unveiling Wuhan's North Side - A Tailored Journey with Must-See Customs Museum

  • Morning (2-3 hours): Indulge in Wuhan's Traditional Breakfast
    • Start your day with a local breakfast experience. Head to Shanhaiguan Road(山海关路), renowned for their diverse selection of Wuhan's traditional breakfast fare (guozǎo).
  • Mid-Morning (2-3 hours): Unveiling the Customs Museum
    • For history enthusiasts, a visit to the Wuhan Customs Museum is a must. Learn about Wuhan's pivotal role in China's maritime trade history.
  • Afternoon (3-4 hours): Discovery of Art and Culture: Wuhan Art Museum
    • Explore the rich world of art and culture at the Wuhan Art Museum. Discover a diverse range of exhibitions featuring contemporary and traditional Chinese art.
  • Evening: Relax and Reflect at Garden Expo Park(园博园)
    • End your journey on a tranquil note with a visit to the Wuhan Garden Expo Park. This expansive park is home to themed gardens, scenic walkways, and serene lakes. Enjoy a leisurely stroll, relax amidst the beautiful landscapes, and reflect on your memorable Wuhan adventure.

Typical Breakfast Food

Hot Dry Noodles

Introduction: Hot dry noodles are a representative breakfast in Wuhan, enjoying nationwide popularity.

Specialty: Made with traditional alkaline noodles, mixed with sesame paste and various seasonings. The noodles are chewy and the sesame paste is fragrant.

Mianwo (Doughnuts)

Introduction: Mianwo is a common breakfast snack with an unassuming appearance but excellent taste.

Specialty: The skin is thin and evenly fried, with a perfect arc. It is golden in color, crispy outside and tender inside, with a delicious glutinous texture.

Sanxian Doupi (Three-Delicacy Bean Skin)

Introduction: Sanxian Doupi is a popular breakfast dish in Wuhan.

Specialty: The fried bean skin is golden in color, wrapped with glutinous rice, mushrooms, pork cubes, and other ingredients. It is crispy outside and tender inside, juicy and savory.

Huanxi Duo (Happy Balls)

Introduction: Huanxi Duo is a round glutinous rice product covered with white sesame seeds.

Specialty: It is crispy outside and tender inside, with a delicious aroma.

Youbing Baoshaomai (Oil Cake Wrapped with Steamed Pork Dumplings)

Introduction: Youbing Baoshaomai is a unique delicacy in Wuhan, combining steamed pork dumplings with oil cakes.

Specialty: The oil cake is thin and crispy, not greasy, and paired with the filling of the steamed pork dumplings, it offers a rich and delicious taste.

Danjiu (Egg Wine)

Introduction: Danjiu is a characteristic breakfast beverage in Wuhan.

Specialty: Made with local eggs, sweet rice wine, and white sugar, it has a refreshing and fragrant taste, often paired with hot dry noodles or fried dough sticks.

Nuomi Baoyoutiao (Glutinous Rice Wrapped with Fried Dough Sticks)

Introduction: Nuomi Baoyoutiao is a street snack in Wuhan.

Specialty: The combination of glutinous rice and fried dough sticks offers a contrasting texture of soft and crispy, making it delicious.

Dousi (Bean Threads)

Introduction: Dousi is a noodle dish made from mung bean flour.

Specialty: It has a soft texture and can be paired with various soup bases or stir-fried dishes, making it a popular breakfast choice in Wuhan.

Shuijiao (Wuhan Wonton)

Introduction: The shuijiao referred to in Wuhan is actually wonton.

Specialty: With rich and diverse fillings, the skin is thin and the meat is generous. The soup base is delicious, making it a common breakfast choice in Wuhan.

Hutangfen (Thick Soup Noodles)

Introduction: Hutangfen is named for its thick and spicy taste, representing the hardworking older generation of dockworkers in Wuhan.

Specialty: Made with small fish broth, it is paired with noodles and seasonings. The taste is rich and spicy, often paired with fried dough sticks.
